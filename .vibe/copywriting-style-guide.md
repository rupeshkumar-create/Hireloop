## WRITING RULES — HOW I LIKE MY EMAILS TO SOUND
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Tone: Conversational, direct, no fluff. Like a message from a smart friend, not a salesperson.
Length: Short. Under 80 words. Get to the point fast.
Opening line: Always reference something specific about the company — not generic. Never start with "I hope this finds you well."
CTA: One soft ask only — a 15-minute call or a simple yes/no question. Never pushy.

Things I NEVER say:
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "Hope you're doing well"
- "I came across your profile"
- "Synergy", "leverage", "bandwidth"
- Long feature lists

Things I ALWAYS do:
- Keep sentences short
- Sound like a human wrote it
- Use their first name once
- End with a question

## OBJECTIONS & HOW I HANDLE THEM
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Objection: "We don't have budget right now."
Response: "$2K is less than one week of an engineer's time. And it's fixed — no surprises."

Objection: "We already have developers."
Response: "Most teams I work with do — but their devs are swamped with the core product. I handle the AI side so your team doesn't have to context-switch."

Objection: "We've tried AI tools before and they didn't work."
Response: "I build custom, not off-the-shelf. If it doesn't work as promised, you don't pay."
