1 in 3 remote jobs you're applying to don't exist.

That's not a hot take. That's the 2026 data.

Ghost listings are everywhere. Companies post roles to build talent pipelines, benchmark salaries, or they just forgot to take them down.

Remote listings suffer worst.

Why? Because remote roles get 2.5x more applicants than in-office ones. Companies can afford to be lazy. They post. They don't hire. You apply. You hear nothing.

Run the numbers:

You send 80 applications.
Maybe 25 were real listings.
Of those 25, maybe 3 get a human review.

And you're sitting there blaming your resume.

The problem isn't your resume.
The problem is where you're looking.

What actually works:

Apply on company career pages directly — skip the aggregators.
Use LinkedIn's "posted in last 24 hours" filter. Fresh listings get fewer applicants.
Ignore listings with no contact name attached.
Target companies hiring 3+ roles at once. That means real headcount budget, not a ghost post.

One thing nobody says out loud:

Manually curated job boards — like We Work Remotely and FlexJobs — have 10x less inventory than LinkedIn.
But the listings are real.

The search isn't broken because you're bad at applying.
It's broken because the pool of real remote listings is much smaller than it looks.
